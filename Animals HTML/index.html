Animal World

There are millions of species, ranging from tiny insects to massive whales, and they're classified into various groups based on shared characteristics

Mammals:

These creatures, including humans, have hair or fur, give birth to live young, and nurse them with milk. They come in all sizes, from tiny bats to enormous elephants.

Reptiles:

This group includes snakes, turtles, lizards, and crocodiles. They typically have scales, lay eggs, and are often ectothermic, relying on external sources to regulate body temperature.

Birds:

Known for feathers, beaks, and laying hard-shelled eggs, birds are found worldwide. They exhibit diverse behaviors and occupy various habitats, from forests to deserts.
